ML Models Write-up & CLV Prediction

Linear Regression

Linear Regression models the relationship between a continuous target variable and one or more predictors by fitting a straight line (or hyperplane) that minimises the sum of squared residuals.

Strengths: Highly interpretable — coefficients directly quantify each feature's impact. Fast to train, works well when relationships are approximately linear.

Limitations: Assumes linearity and is sensitive to outliers; may underfit complex non-linear patterns common in retail spend data.

CLV application: Predicts a household's total future spend from RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) features and demographics as a baseline model.

Random Forest

Random Forest is an ensemble of decision trees, each trained on a random bootstrap sample and a random feature subset. Final predictions are averaged (regression) or voted (classification) across all trees.

Strengths: Handles non-linear interactions and high-dimensional features naturally; robust to outliers; built-in feature importance scores.

Limitations: Less interpretable than a single tree; can be slow to train on very large datasets; may overfit noisy data without tuning.

CLV application: Captures non-linear relationships between household size, income, and spending patterns that Linear Regression misses.

Gradient Boosting

Gradient Boosting builds trees sequentially; each new tree corrects the residual errors of the ensemble so far by descending the gradient of the loss function.

Strengths: Often achieves the highest predictive accuracy of the three; handles mixed feature types; built-in regularisation (learning rate, subsampling).

Limitations: More hyperparameters to tune than Random Forest; sequential nature means longer training times; can overfit if the learning rate is too high.

CLV application: Best model when accuracy matters most — e.g., prioritising the top 10% of high-value customers for targeted promotions.

Business Insight

High-CLV households can be identified using Gradient Boosting predictions. Kroger can use these scores to prioritise personalised coupon offers, loyalty tier upgrades, and targeted email campaigns — focusing retention budget on customers who generate the most long-term revenue.

Low-CLV but high-frequency households represent cross-sell opportunities: they visit often but spend little per trip, suggesting they may shop other retailers for major categories.
